History of Pasco, Washington:

Pasco grew to be a vital railroad town in the years before World War II, including what is considered the beginning of United Airlines in 1926. The war brought the Manhattan Project, the United States' development of the atomic bomb, to the nearby Hanford Site.

Demographics for Pasco, Washington:

  • Race and ethnicity: 55.5% Hispanic, 36.8% White, 2.9% two or more races
  • Population: 77,818 residents
  • Median age: 29.9
  • Gender: 50.35% male, 49.65% female
  • Citizenship: 74.89% US-born citizens, 10.56% non-US-born citizens, 14.55% non-citizens
  • Age groups: Largest age group is between 25 and 64 years old

Population Growth

Pasco grew by more than a third as 20,000 residents joined the city between 2010 and 2022. With a current population estimated at 80,180

Attractions in Pasco, Washington:

  • Franklin County Historical Society & Museum: Small museum with local history exhibits, including old photographs and artifacts
  • Sacajawea State Park Interpretive Center: Small museum in a beautiful park
  • REACH Museum: Center for the study of regional history, geology, science, and technology
  • East Benton County Historical Society Museum: Local heritage museum with ancient Native American petroglyphs and a gift shop
  • Pasco Aviation Museum: Museum with cool displays
  • CPCCo Planetarium: Educational and fun planetarium
